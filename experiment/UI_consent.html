Research Study Title: Multimodal Neuroimaging of Perceptual Inference
Protocol number: 2021-7130
Researchers responsible for the research study: Sylvain Baillet, Ph.D. and Max Levinson

INTRODUCTION

You have been invited to participate in this behavioral study. Please read this consent information attentively: it explains the procedure and your rights to information, confidentiality and of withdrawing from the study at any time.

PURPOSE OF THE RESEARCH STUDY

This study seeks to better understand human sensory perception, including vision and hearing. For this research study, we will recruit approximately 500 participants, men and women, aged between 18-65.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RESEARCH PROCEDURES

This research study will take place on your home computer. Your participation will include one session lasting between 10-30 minutes. In this study you will participate in one or more simple computer-based perception tasks on your home computer's internet browser. Each task is expected to last no longer than 10 minutes. You will view pictures or hear recordings and answer questions about what you perceive.

BENEFITS ASSOCIATED WITH THE RESEARCH STUDY

There is no direct benefit to you in participating in this research besides monetary compensation. However, we hope that the study results will contribute to the advancement of scientific knowledge.

RISKS ASSOCIATED WITH THE RESEARCH STUDY

We do not foresee any risks associated with this study.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION AND THE RIGHT TO WITHDRAW

Your participation in this study is voluntary. Therefore, you may refuse to participate. You may also withdraw from the ongoing project at any time, without giving any reason, by closing your internet browser. Any useful data obtained prior to your withdrawal will be kept on record and used for research purposes and data analysis. This data will be anonymous and cannot be used to identify you.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Confidentiality of your personal information will be protected: the only persons who will be aware of your identity and demographic information will be the principal investigator and his co-investigators. Only anonymized data will be used for publication purposes, and this anonymized data might be made available in a data repository to other researchers for subsequent studies. Study data will be stored by the investigators for at least 10 years.

FUNDING OF THE RESEARCH PROJECT

The researcher and the institution have received funding from NSERC Discovery, Canada Research Chair, and Healthy Brains for Healthy Lives to conduct this research project.

CONFLICT OF INTERESTS

The researchers have no conflict of interest to declare.

COMPENSATION

You will receive a total compensation in the amount of $15 CAD per hour, proportional to the estimated amount of time required to complete your assigned task(s). If you withdraw before study completion, you will receive an amount proportional to your participation. However, if you fail to follow study instructions, your compensation may be withheld.

SHARING STUDY RESULTS

Results from this study will be presented at conferences and published in journals.

CONTACT INFORMATION

For enquiries about the project the principal investigator Sylvain Baillet can be reached by phone: 514-398-5469 or email sylvain.baillet@mcgill.ca. You are also informed that you should not sign this consent form without having had a chance to thoroughly examine the consent form.

OVERVIEW OF ETHICAL ASPECTS OF THE RESEARCH

The McGill University Health Centre Research Ethics Board reviewed this research and is responsible for monitoring the study.
